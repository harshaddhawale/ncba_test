The NCBA offers academic institutions and companies an access to the latest research and innovation trends within their respective fields of expertise as well as provide inspiration on tendencies within new technology, product innovation and innovation methods.The NCBA can:

  • Help develop ideas into new competitive products or services.
  • Provide inputs and advise for solving problems with product development and innovation.
  • Provide access to a comprehensive network of researchers from universities, technological service providers as well as other private companies.
  • Provide overview of and contact to the public research and innovation promotion system.
  • Provide specific professional resources for relieving companies of the burden to invest in manpower or the latest technological knowledge themselves.
  • Be a stepping stone for an internationalization of companies.

CEO (Creating Entrepreneurial Opportunities) classroom programme of NCBA, engages the students and faculty of Academic Institutions in a hands-on learning experience embedded in the context of businesses. Financed by the contributions of local investors, the CEO class offers students access to mentoring relationships with business leaders and the opportunity to design and start a real business, with the goal of helping young entrepreneurs see and take advantage of the possibilities for success in their local communities.

The NCBA recognises the importance of research, science and innovation for increasing productivity and wellbeing to achieve long term economic growth for the economy and community and to enable. Research is a key contributor to improving India’s productivity over the longer term.NCBA has an increasing focus on showcasing or measuring the societal benefits from research, and a need for better coordination in reporting and promoting the impact of these research outcomes. This will become increasingly important in a tight fiscal government environment where returns on investment in research will need to be demonstrated in terms of environmental, economic and social impact. For these reasons and others, key stakeholders including government, industry and the community require more information on the benefits derived from investment in these research activities.The working groups of NCBA work on the following principles to underpin the measurement of research impact.

  • Acknowledge that excellent research underpins impact.
  • Promote understanding through use of common language and terms associated with research impact.
  • Respect the diversity in research disciplines/sectors in demonstrating research impact.
  • Cooperate in developing a set of common, cost effective and efficient parameters for data collection and reporting.
  • Adopt a consultative approach with stakeholders in regards to implementing impact reporting in support of future research investments.
  • Encourage, recognize and reward positive behavior in planning, monitoring and evaluating research impact.

The NCBA’s Industrial Internship Program trains the students in the real-world knowledge and skills necessary to be successful in the industrial research laboratory - and at the interview table. We believe an advanced degree should help you land a job and prepare you for a successful career. It turns out at many companies agree with us to do so. These internships are the best way to bridge the gap between Business and Academia situations. Internships can help to develop participants’ experience by learning the ropes from more experienced professionals.

The NCBA conducts the market research to analyze the project and methods that have been developed for university – business cooperation. These researches are done by looking at the impact of the factors that facilitate or inhibit university-industry cooperation. We understand the status quo in science (teaching, further education, research and technology transfer) and have created some new concepts (Partnering, Science to Business Marketing, integration of stakeholders (customers), in the development of university (teaching, further education, research and transfer) – industry cooperation as a response. We conduct research to understand current and future markets for your research results. This includes, but is not limited to:

  • Customer requirements and inclination
  • Market size and potential
  • Competitor analysis
  • Usability and market tests
